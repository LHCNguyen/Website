Master Your TOEIC Skills

Prepare effectively for the TOEIC exam with detailed strategies and practice for Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking sections.

Listening

The TOEIC Listening segment is 45 minutes long and includes 100 multiple-choice questions. It tests your ability to understand spoken English in various contexts.

Test Structure

  • Part 1: Match 6 recorded descriptions to images (10 minutes).
  • Part 2: Answer 25 short questions or statements.
  • Part 3: Answer 39 questions based on 13 dialogs.
  • Part 4: Answer 30 questions based on 10 monologs.

Strategy

Recordings play only once, so listen carefully and move on quickly. Blank answers count as incorrect, so always guess if unsure. Expect American, British, Canadian, or Australian accents.

Reading

The TOEIC Reading segment is 75 minutes long with 100 multiple-choice questions. It tests your ability to understand written English in various formats.

Test Structure

  • Part 1: Complete 30 sentences with 4 options each.
  • Part 2: Fill in blanks in 4 texts (16 questions).
  • Part 3: Answer 29 questions on 10 texts + 25 questions on paired texts.

Strategy

Manage your time wisely; don’t linger on difficult questions. Blank answers are incorrect, so guess when unsure. Eliminate wrong answers to improve your guessing odds.

Writing

The TOEIC Writing segment is 1 hour long with 8 prompts. It tests your ability to communicate effectively in written English on a computer.

Test Structure

  • Part 1: Write 5 sentences based on pictures (8 minutes).
  • Part 2: Write 2 email responses (10 minutes each).
  • Part 3: Write 1 opinion essay (30 minutes).

Strategy

Always write something, even if unsure, as blank answers score zero. Practice typing on a QWERTY keyboard to save time during the test.

Speaking

The TOEIC Speaking segment is 20 minutes long with 11 prompts. It tests your ability to speak fluently and accurately in English on a computer.

Test Structure

  • Part 1: Read 2 texts aloud (45 seconds each).
  • Part 2: Describe 1 picture (45 seconds).
  • Part 3: Answer 3 everyday questions.
  • Part 4: Answer 3 questions based on background info.
  • Part 5: Propose a solution to a problem (60 seconds).
  • Part 6: Express an opinion on a topic (60 seconds).

Strategy

Speak clearly and complete each answer fully. Blank answers score zero, so always say something in English. Gestures won’t help, so focus on verbal clarity.
